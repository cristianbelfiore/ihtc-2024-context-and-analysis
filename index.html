This page provides supplementary material for our investigation of the IHTC 2024 benchmark instances. The interactive figures are organized into thematic sections, allowing the reader to explore instance-space patterns, the effect of weighting choices, and the role of critical resource saturation.

Instance Space Projection (Section 4.1, Fig. 3)

The projection of instances in the (Z1, Z2) space is obtained through dimensionality reduction via PILOT, and highlights the relationship between instance characteristics and algorithmic performance. The separating boundary between easy and hard instances is approximated through logistic regression, allowing a clearer interpretation of instance difficulty across the space.

Feature Distributions in the Instance Space (Section 4.1, Fig. 4)

In the figures below, it is possible to observe the trends over the instances of three selected features: (a) total operating theatre availability, (b) total surgeon availability, and (c) median number of incompatible rooms per patient.

In each plot, all instances are projected onto the two-dimensional space defined by the coordinates (Z1, Z2). The values of the considered features are encoded through a color scale, ranging from lower values (in purple) to higher values (in yellow).

(a) Operating theatre availability across the instance space (minutes).
(b) Surgeon availability across the instance space (minutes).
(c) Median number of incompatible rooms per patient across the instance space.

Best Performing Algorithm per Instance (Section 4.1, Fig. 5)

In the figure below, each instance is colored according to the algorithm achieving the best performance.
